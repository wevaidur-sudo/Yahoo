Is the app professionally reliable to trade

Honest answer: not yet, but closer than most retail tools.

Here's what the audit found:

✅ What works correctly
Signal engine — The buy/sell signals are deterministic and mathematically sound. RSI, MACD, VWAP, and ORB are all implemented correctly with no lookahead bias. The ORB has a 15-minute formation window (9:30–9:45 ET) to prevent false early breakouts. Only setup types that passed backtesting are allowed through.

Options math — Black-Scholes uses real inputs (live IV from Yahoo, live T-bill rate for risk-free rate). Probability of Profit is computed via a 6,000-step integration of the lognormal terminal price distribution — not an AI guess. The PoP gates (25% floor, 99% ceiling, live IV required) prevent garbage from surfacing.

Data staleness — There's a 15-minute staleness check. If quote data is stale, the UI shows a warning banner.

Error handling — Promise.allSettled on all data fetches means partial failures (missing news, no options chain) degrade gracefully rather than crashing.

⚠️ What makes it conditional right now
No position sizing — The app takes your dollar amount and scales contracts, but there's no portfolio-level guardrail. A professional system caps risk at 1–2% of total account per trade. This app has no concept of your total account size.

Slippage and commissions not modeled — Max profit/loss numbers assume perfect fill at mid-price. Real fills on options, especially spreads, are worse. For a $100 budget this matters a lot — a $1.50 commission round-trip is 3% of capital before the trade does anything.

Yahoo Finance rate limiting — The app makes 5 API calls per symbol and scans up to ~97 symbols. Yahoo enforces undocumented IP-based limits. Under heavy use or during market open rush, some fetches will get throttled and return stale or partial data silently.

No order routing — The app tells you what to trade, not how to get filled. Limit order vs. market order vs. midpoint strategy for a vertical spread matters enormously in illiquid names.
